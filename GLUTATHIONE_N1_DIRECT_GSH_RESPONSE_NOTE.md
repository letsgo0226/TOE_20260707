# Glutathione N=1 Direct GSH Response Note

## Status

This note records a personal experiential observation and translates it into a bounded, safety-preserving protocol.

It does **not** claim that glutathione is universally superior to NAC, GlyNAC, or GlyNAC-ET for all people, conditions, doses, formulations, or clinical outcomes.

## User Observation

The user reports that, from personal use, direct glutathione feels subjectively stronger in antioxidant effect than NAC or GlyNAC-ET.

```text
PersonalResponse:
  Direct_GSH > NAC / GlyNAC-ET
```

## Safe Interpretation

The observation may be interpreted as an N=1 response pattern:

```text
Direct glutathione = finished molecule / direct support
NAC / GlyNAC = precursor support / substrate pathway
```

A stronger subjective response to direct glutathione may arise from:

1. formulation differences,
2. absorption differences,
3. timing differences,
4. baseline nutritional status,
5. individual metabolism,
6. whether the limiting factor is precursor availability or immediately usable glutathione.

## Boundary

Subjective response is meaningful as personal data, but it is not identical to universal proof.

```text
N=1 experience is a signal.
It is not a randomized trial.
It may guide personal tracking,
but it should not replace medical judgment.
```

```text
If lifestyle load remains high:
  supplements alone may not close the loop.
```

## Safety Rules

1. Do not stack many high-dose antioxidants without tracking.
2. Do not use supplements as a replacement for needed medical care.
3. Record dose, timing, formulation, sleep, mood, diet, and side effects.
4. Discuss supplement use with a clinician if there are chronic illnesses, regular medications, liver/kidney issues, asthma, psychiatric treatment, or upcoming procedures.
5. Treat the best protocol as adaptive rather than absolute.
